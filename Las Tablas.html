Carnaval Las Tablas

The Rivalry Turned Into Celebration

This vibrant and lively festival, which is one of Panama's best known cultural events, lasts four days in March before Ash Wednesday each year. This yearly event was first celebrated in the 19th century as a rivalry between the Calle Arriba and Calle Abajo religious groups, who both created songs and dances to battle other groups four days before Catholics begin Lent. The carnival now symbolizes harmony and unity as a result of the hard forgiveness of this big fight. To remember the early stages of this historic event, traditional folk songs and dances were performed throughout the celebration. People go from all over the world to enjoy the vibrant parades that mark this in the majority of the cities and villages in Panama. The festival takes the name from the city where it takes place, Las Tablas.

The two tuna parades in street attire are held after the water, blue, and shaving cream battles in the center plaza in the morning. Fireworks everywhere , cheerful dancing, and street performers keep the festivities going throughout the night. During the carnival, families enjoy time together while enjoying drinks and food. Don't miss this exciting event full of cultural richness and happiness.
