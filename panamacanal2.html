Panama Canal

The Waterway That Changed Panama

The Panama Canal is a human-made waterway that connects the Atlantic and Pacific ocean through the isthmus of Panama. The canal is 40 miles long from the start to the end of the waterway. The estimated time it takes for a ship to pass from side to side is of 10 hours. Ships from any country can pass without restriction, even Russia with all the restrictions that the country has passed in the world due to the invasion of Ukraine. The Panama Canal is considered one of the seven wonders of the modern world.


The canal is an essential part of Panama's economy taking 10% of Panama’s GDP. This canal represents a lot of Panama’s economy but it also giganticly marks the history of the country, especially the independence of Panama. The Panama Canal was first started to be built in 1882 by the French when Panama’s independence from Spain had already occurred and when the nation was part of La Gran Colombia. The French company was allied with Colombia to make the Panama Canal, but later the company went bankrupt. When La Gran Colombia broke down and Panama was still of the Republic of Colombia the U.S took control of the construction and hoped to agree with Colombia, but Colombia rejected it. Taking advantage of the situation, Panama, rebelled against Colombia and declared its independence in 1904 with the help of the U.S. Later, the U.S gave full control of the canal to Panama.


The Panama Canal shares multiple things to do for tourists. Even though a lot of tourists go especially to see one of the seven wonders of the world, they have more things to do in the surrounding of the canal. Tourists visit places like the Miraflores Visitor Center, The Gatun Locks and Gatun Lake, Casa Casco Viejo, and the Museum of the canal. Miraflores is special if you want to give a look at the history of the canal and its unique diversity or you can observe from a balcony how ships transit through the canal. In the Gatun Locks and Gatun Lake, you can have an adventure admiring one of the Canal’s water suppliers. If you just want to chill and be in another ambient, you can opt for Casco Viejo. In Casco Viejo, you will see the special Spanish architecture giving you the airs of Venice. For visitors that really want to know everything about the Panama Canal, you can go to the Canal’s museum.
